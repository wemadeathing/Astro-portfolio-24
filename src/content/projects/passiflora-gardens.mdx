## Project Overview

I worked with Passiflora Gardens, a Cape Town landscaping and horticultural services company, to build their brand identity and digital presence from scratch. What made this project different was the level of iteration — the business was still refining its direction, so the brand had to evolve alongside it. Multiple rounds, constant feedback, and strategic pivots shaped the final result.

## The Challenge

**Evolving Business Direction**
The owner was still figuring out their exact market positioning. The brand couldn't be rigid — it needed to adapt as Passiflora shifted focus between residential gardens, lawn care, and commercial landscape maintenance.

**Dual Audience**
The identity had to appeal to homeowners wanting beautiful gardens and commercial clients needing professional maintenance. Two very different audiences, one brand.

**Establishing Credibility**
A new landscaping business in Cape Town's competitive market needed to look established and trustworthy from day one.

## Process & Approach

### Brand Development

I ran a brand sprint with the owner to establish core values, audience, and positioning. Then I worked through several design directions. This wasn't a one-and-done deliverable — we iterated multiple times as the owner's vision for the business crystallized.

Each round of feedback sharpened the identity. When the business pivoted to emphasize commercial landscape maintenance alongside residential work, I adjusted the messaging and visual approach to support that shift. The final brand system works across both contexts without feeling generic.

### Website Build

I built the website on WordPress, structuring it to clearly communicate their full range of services — lawn care, garden consultations, and commercial landscape maintenance. The site positions them as horticultural experts while keeping the path to booking a consultation straightforward.

Key priorities were clear service descriptions, professional presentation for commercial prospects, and an approachable feel for residential customers. The consultation request system was the main conversion point.

## Results & Impact

**Brand That Adapted**
Delivered a brand identity that evolved through multiple iterations rather than being forced into a predetermined direction. The final result genuinely represents where the business landed.

**Professional Presence**
Passiflora Gardens went from no brand presence to a cohesive identity and live website in three weeks. They now compete visually with established players in Cape Town's landscaping market.

**Lead Generation**
The website serves as an effective lead generation tool with clear service pages and an easy consultation request system driving inbound enquiries.

## Key Learnings

- Iterative brand work with an evolving business requires flexibility, but produces a more authentic result than a rigid process.
- Designing for dual audiences (residential and commercial) means finding the overlap in trust signals rather than trying to speak to each separately.
- WordPress remains a solid choice for service businesses that need to manage their own content updates without developer involvement.
- Close collaboration with the business owner through strategic shifts builds stronger brands than delivering a fixed package and walking away.
